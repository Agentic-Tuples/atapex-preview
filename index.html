The Problem

The GM's and Ops Director's Dilemma

You run production, finance, procurement, and compliance — often simultaneously. Your ERP captures transactions. It doesn't tell you what to do about them.

⚠️

EDI changes hit at midnight

A customer reschedules a delivery at 11pm. Nobody catches it until 9am. By then you've already committed the labor, tooling, and materials.

📊

Production Plan to achieve the Financial Forecast has changed again

Your indirect rate crept 4 points over 6 weeks. No alert. No flag. You find out when the customer asks why the program is over budget.

🔗

Supplier signals go unread

A key supplier has missed partial deliveries three weeks running. It's buried in PO acknowledgment emails. No one connected the pattern until it became a line-stop.

"What would you have done with that information at midnight instead of 8am?"
— The question APEX answers every single day

How It Works

We don't need your ERP to be clean.

APEX is document-first. PDFs, EDI flat files, reconciliation spreadsheets — the data you already have, in the formats it already exists in.

Step 01
📁

Connect Your Documents

Drop in PDFs, EDI files, and spreadsheets. No ERP integration required. No IT project. No data cleaning sprint.

Step 02
🤖

Agents Run Overnight

12 specialized agents work while you sleep — monitoring production, supply chain, and financial signals across your entire operation.

Step 03
☀️

Decisions Ready at 6AM

You open APEX at morning standup to a Command Center briefing with ranked findings, root causes, and recommended actions.

Step 04
✅

Act, Don't React

Approve recommendations, delegate actions, or dig deeper — all from one interface. Lead with clarity instead of catching up all day.
